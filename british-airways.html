Problem

Thousands of opinions, no single view

Airlines live and die by customer perception, but the raw signal is messy. British Airways passengers leave detailed public reviews spanning many years, each scoring the airline across several service dimensions and written as free-form text.

In that raw form the feedback is almost impossible to act on: there is no easy way to see which parts of the experience consistently disappoint, whether satisfaction differs by aircraft or route, or how sentiment has shifted over time. The insight exists — it is just locked inside unstructured data.

Objective

The question to answer

Consolidate years of customer reviews into one analysis that pinpoints which service dimensions drive satisfaction, how sentiment varies by aircraft and region, and where BA should focus improvement — presented so a non-technical stakeholder can explore it themselves.

Data & approach

From raw web pages to a decision tool

01

Collect

Scraped 1,000+ public customer reviews covering 7+ years using Python (requests & BeautifulSoup), capturing both the written review and its structured star ratings.

02

Clean & structure

Parsed each review into a tidy dataset — separating the six rating dimensions, standardising dates, and extracting fields like aircraft type, traveller type, and route for segmentation.

03

Analyse

Ran exploratory and statistical analysis to compare average scores across dimensions, aircraft, regions, and time, and to find where ratings cluster high or low.

04

Visualise

Built an interactive Tableau dashboard with filters so stakeholders can slice satisfaction by dimension, aircraft, and region without touching the underlying data.
